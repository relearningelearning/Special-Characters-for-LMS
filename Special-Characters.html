Special Characters in eLearning

What are Special Characters?

Special characters are symbols that extend beyond standard letters and numbers you see displayed on the screen. They include punctuation marks, currency signs, mathematical operators, typographic symbols, and even emojis. These characters help to provide additional meaning, clarity, or context within text. For example, symbols like the ampersand (&) are used to replace the word 'and,' while others, like the copyright mark (Â©), indicate ownership. In eLearning, using these special characters effectively can greatly improve the readability, context, consistency, accessibility and the overall experience of the learning process.

Understanding Unicode

Unicode is a universal character encoding standard that assigns a unique number (or code point) to every character, regardless of the platform, language, or program being used. In HTML, these Unicode characters can be represented using either a numeric or named code. For example, the copyright symbol can be represented as `©` (a named code: &copy;) or `©` (a numeric code: &#169;). This ensures unique web content is consistent, accessible, and compatible across different devices and browsers.

Why use this Resource

This resource is designed to guide educators and content creators in using special characters effectively within Learning Management Systems (LMS). By using the correct Unicode symbols and understanding their importance, educators can ensure that their course content is both precise and accessible, leading to a richer learning experience for all students.

Whether it's accurately displaying mathematical operators, creating proper chemical formulas, or simply improving navigation with clear visual symbols, this guide will help you special characters encountered in eLearning and empower you to discover more you can add to your curriculum delivery.


What about Emojis?

Yes, emojis are considered special characters and can be useful in eLearning when used appropriately. They can add emotional context, emphasize points, and make content fun and engaging. However, it's important to use emojis sparingly and ensure they do not interfere with the clarity/context of the content or accessibility tools such as screen readers. When used thoughtfully, emojis can enhance the learning experience by adding an extra layer of meaning and playfulness.
